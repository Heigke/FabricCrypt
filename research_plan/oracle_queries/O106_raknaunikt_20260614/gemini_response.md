### 6. How Physical/Reservoir PUFs Achieve Device-Specific Computation

Your question about reservoir computing is astute, as it gets to the heart of the matter.

*   **Mechanism:** Reservoir computing (RC) and physical PUFs leverage high-dimensional, nonlinear dynamics. An input perturbs a complex, disordered physical system (the "reservoir"). The response is a rich, high-dimensional state vector that is a complex function of the input. This function, `f(input) = state_vector`, is determined by the microscopic physical details of the reservoir (e.g., dopant positions, grain boundaries, parasitic capacitances). These details are unclonable manufacturing variations.
*   **Device-Specificity:** A linear readout is trained to map this device-specific state vector to a desired output. Because the function `f()` is unique to each device, a readout trained on Device A will fail on Device B, as the state vectors for the same input will be completely different. The *computation* is the transformation `f()` performed by the physical system, and that transformation is inherently device-bound.
*   **Citations & Mapping to APU:**
    *   **Krause et al. (2023, *Neuromorphic Comput. Eng.* 3 034001):** This work uses networks of memristive nanowires. The device-specific computation arises from the unique connectivity and switching thresholds of the nanowire junctions, which are a product of a self-assembly process. The "computation" is the path the electrical signal takes through this complex resistive network.
    *   **Sarantoglou et al. (2024, arXiv:2405.11448):** This paper (the year is 2024, not 2025) uses photonic integrated circuits. The "computation" is how input light scatters and interferes within a disordered waveguide structure. The specific scattering pattern is exquisitely sensitive to nanoscale fabrication imperfections, making the light transformation `f(input)` unique to the chip.
*   **Applicability:** Your APU's PDN *is* a reservoir, albeit a simpler one than those designed for RC. Your `u·v` term is a low-order nonlinearity. The die-specific "computation" is likely hidden in the higher-order dynamics and transient responses—the very phenomena explored in Idea #2 and #4. You are not yet measuring the full richness of the reservoir's response.

### 7. Brutal Verdict and Highest-Probability Experiment

**Verdict:** "Räkna unikt" is achievable on a locked commodity APU, but you are looking in the wrong place. You have fixated on the *transferability of the readout model* instead of the *die-specificity of the physical parameters*. The generic transfer proves the underlying physics (`P ≈ I²R`) is the same on both dies. This is not a flaw; it is a feature that allows for a differential measurement.

You should stop trying to make the XOR readout *fail* to transfer. Instead, you must prove that a parameter of the computation itself is a stable, die-specific identifier.

The single highest-probability experiment is a refined version of **Idea #2: Ratio-Metric Measurement of the Nonlinear Coefficient**. This directly tackles the thermal confound and provides a quantifiable, physical basis for die-specificity.

### **Protocol: Ratio-Metric Thermal-Sweep Characterization of the u·v Coefficient**

**Objective:** To demonstrate that the ratio of the nonlinear power-contention coefficient (`k_uv`) to a linear power-draw coefficient (`k_u`) is a temperature-stable, die-specific quantity.

**Pre-registered Acceptance Criteria:**
1.  A regression model of telemetry channel `C` of the form `y_C = β₀ + β_u·u + β_v·v + β_uv·(u·v) + ε` must show a statistically significant non-zero `β_uv` coefficient on both dies (confirms the physical product term exists).
2.  For at least one channel `C`, the 95% confidence intervals for the ratio `R_C = β_uv / β_u` must be non-overlapping between `ikaros` and `daedalus` across a range of operating temperatures.
3.  The coefficient of variation of the ratio `R_C(T)` across the temperature sweep must be significantly lower than that of the raw `β_uv(T)` coefficient, demonstrating successful thermal compensation.

**Experimental Protocol:**

1.  **Thermal Control:** Identify a method to stabilize die temperature at specific setpoints (e.g., 75°C, 85°C, 95°C). This can be done by adjusting a background load and waiting for thermal equilibrium. Do not proceed until the target temperature is stable (±1°C). This is the most critical step.

2.  **Stimulus Design:**
    *   Create a randomized but repeatable sequence of `(u, v)` bit-pairs. `u` is the GPU burst command (0 or 1), `v` is the CPU burst command (0 or 1).
    *   The sequence must contain balanced numbers of `(0,0)`, `(0,1)`, `(1,0)`, and `(1,1)` to allow for robust regression fitting.
    *   Keep the duty cycle low to minimize thermal drift during a single run. A run at one temperature setpoint should be short (<30 seconds).

3.  **Data Acquisition:**
    *   For each die (`ikaros`, `daedalus`):
    *   For each temperature setpoint (`T` in {75, 85, 95}):
        *   Stabilize the die at temperature `T`.
        *   Run the stimulus sequence and record the full vector of telemetry channels time-synced with the `u` and `v` commands.
        *   Repeat 5 times to gather statistics.

4.  **Analysis:**
    *   For each die, temperature, and telemetry channel, perform a multiple linear regression: `telemetry_reading ~ u + v + u:v` (where `u:v` is the interaction term).
    *   This will yield the coefficients `β_u(die, T, C)`, `β_v(die, T, C)`, and `β_uv(die, T, C)`.
    *   Calculate the ratio `R_C(die, T) = β_uv(die, T, C) / β_u(die, T, C)`. The `β_u` term acts as an internal reference for that channel's sensitivity to a known power draw, effectively canceling out temperature-dependent gain changes that affect both terms.

5.  **Adjudication:**
    *   Plot `R_C(T)` with 95% confidence intervals for `ikaros` and `daedalus` on the same graph.
    *   If the confidence bands for the two dies are clearly separated for a specific channel `C`, you have demonstrated that the *physical constant of computation* is die-specific and you have controlled for the thermal confound.
    *   This result, `R_C_ikaros ≠ R_C_daedalus`, is direct evidence of `RÄKNA UNIKT`.

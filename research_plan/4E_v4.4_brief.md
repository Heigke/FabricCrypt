## 1. Executive summary

v4.4 ships **two network-level positive headlines** and **one honest negative** on the compact model.

- **Headline A (network):** Hyperdimensional Computing on NS-RAM-encoded features reaches **84.09 % accuracy on UCI-HAR** at N = 16 384 dimensions, n = 10 seeds, std ≈ 0.3 pp, at an estimated **~35 nJ per inference** (neuron-core surrogate energy; sensor + classifier excluded).
- **Headline B (resource):** NS-RAM physical noise as Bayesian RNG. ESS = 1.03 × `numpy.random` over 10 k Metropolis-Hastings steps; **NIST SP800-22 subset 5 / 5 PASS** (monobit, runs, longest-run, binary-matrix-rank 32 × 32, DFT). Negative control (all-zeros) fails 0/5 as expected.
- **Honest negative:** v4.3's "**z304 0.99 dec** cell-wide DC fit" headline is **confirmed spurious**. After fixing two real bugs (R-20 BJT collector wire, R-29 Vth/tox) the **physics-correct cell-wide baseline is 4.08 dec**. The 0.99 dec figure was numerical compensation for the broken BJT topology, not a legitimate fit — reverting either patch restores the old "good" number, i.e. v4.3 was scoring a wrong physics path against a real silicon target.
- **Framing:** NS-RAM remains an **IP-licensable spiking-neuron-core macro for standard-CMOS MCU/SoC integration** (3/3 oracle convergence, 4A). v4.4 tightens this framing.

KWS (Speech Commands 12-class) was attempted and **remains at chance (~8 %)**. Reported as a negative, not erased.

## 3. Model-fit quality — HONEST status (the v4.3 retraction)

**v4.3 claim:** z304 median log-RMSE per row = 0.99 dec on Sebas's 33-bias DC sweep.

**What we discovered (R-13 through R-31 arc, 2026-05-13 → 2026-05-14, ~6 hours, 11 z-experiments, 6 oracle waves):**

### 3.1 Two real bugs were fixed

1. **R-20 — BJT collector miswire.** `nsram_cell_2T.py` L530 had `Vbc = Vb − Vd` instead of `Vbc = Vb − Vsint` (sint = source-side internal node). Pyport parasitic NPN was reading the wrong collector potential. After fix, reverting the patch costs **+1.8 dec** cell-wide — i.e. the broken wire was *what made the old number look good*. Pyport had been silently compensating for a topology error.
2. **R-29 — `patch_model_values` stale entries.** `lpe0 = 1.74e-7` and `toxe = 3e-9` in the patch table did not match Sebas's BSIM card values (`lpe0 = 1.2439e-7`, `toxe = 4e-9`). Vth shifted **−96 mV**, Ids rose **+3.3 dec** at the flagship bias (V_G1 = 0.6, V_G2 = 0.2, V_d = 2.0 V). Channel patch landed as predicted at the local probe.

### 3.2 The cell-wide outcome is honestly worse than v4.3

With both fixes applied (z346):

| Branch | Median log-RMSE (dec) |
|---|---|
| Cell-wide (33 rows) | **4.08** |
| V_G1 = 0.20 | **2.32** (correct subthreshold regime) |
| V_G1 = 0.40 | 3.74 |
| V_G1 = 0.60 | 5.42 (high gate-overdrive still broken) |

MASTER_FIX_PLAN gate < 0.7 dec **not met** at any branch. R-29's local-probe prediction "Vth fix → 2.6 dec cell-wide gain" was **partially falsified**: true at V_G1 = 0.20 (−0.78 dec), flat at V_G1 = 0.4 / 0.6 — channel fix necessary but not sufficient at high overdrive.

### 3.3 Snapback shape — qualitatively right, amplitude off

R-26 ngspice work confirmed the Iii path with `lalpha0 → 0`, `alpha0 × 10` reproduces the snapback **shape** at flagship bias. In ngspice the patch lifts the curve ~3 dec. **In pyport the same patch lifts only −0.17 dec** because pyport's underlying Ids_M1 is ~3 dec below ngspice's even at identical OPs. Architecture is consistent (R-25: py ≡ ng under injected OP), but the BSIM4 channel evaluator inside pyport is 2–3 dec light at high overdrive.

### 3.4 What we are NOT claiming

- HDC and RNG results do **not** depend on cell DC fit < 1 dec — they use NS-RAM as feature-generator / noise source; row-level DC accuracy is not on the critical path.
- Snapback peak law is **not** quantitatively matched at cell level (sign error: +0.611 model vs −0.625 silicon on V_G2 slope).
- v4.3's energy headline (10× silicon-energy floor) is **not** retracted; unaffected.
- v4.3's three-source 0.51-dec triangulation is **not** retracted — it is a per-V_G1-branch average, not the same metric as the 33-row median.

## 4. Network results — where v4.4 wins

### 4.1 HDC on UCI-HAR (Headline A)

NS-RAM-encoded feature hypervectors (32-level Q encoding, V_G1 = V_G2 = 0.3 V, V_d ∈ [0.5, 2.0] V), k-NN over class prototypes. Energy is the per-inference neuron-core surrogate (sensor + classifier excluded).

| Configuration | n | Mean acc | Energy / inf |
|---|---|---|---|
| **N = 16 384, σ = 0.00** | 10 | **84.09 %** | ~35 nJ |
| N = 16 384, σ = 0.05 | 10 | ~84 % | ~35 nJ |
| N = 8192, σ = 0.00 | 4 | ~83 % | ~18 nJ |
| N = 1024 | 4 | ~80 % | ~2.3 nJ |
| N = 64 | 4 | ~59 % | — |

Saturation between N = 8192 and N = 16 384 (~85 % ceiling). Noise tolerance at locked n = 10 is statistically identical at σ = 0 and σ = 0.05 — we no longer claim NS-RAM noise *helps* HDC, only that it doesn't hurt at this N (correcting an earlier overclaim flagged in O49).

### 4.2 Bayesian RNG (Headline B)

`results/z296b_nist_randomness/summary.json`. ESS = 1.03 × `numpy.random` on a fixed test posterior over 10 k MH steps. NIST SP800-22 subset on 1 Mbit streams:

| Test | NS-RAM p | np.random p | zero-control |
|---|---|---|---|
| monobit | 0.904 | 0.679 | 0.0 |
| runs | 0.981 | 0.077 | 0.0 |
| longest-run | 0.915 | 0.496 | 4.4 × 10⁻²²⁰ |
| matrix-rank 32×32 | 0.097 | 0.118 | 0.0 |
| DFT spectral | 0.0435 | 0.331 | 0.0 |

5/5 PASS at α = 0.01. Negative control 0/5 as expected. All three 4D oracles flagged this as the most paradigm-relevant single finding in the project — physical noise from a standard-CMOS analog cell is competitive with a software PRNG on every tested NIST measure, with **non-cryptographic** scope.

## 5. Commercial framing (unchanged from 4A consensus)

NS-RAM is best pitched as a **0.01–0.02 mm² spiking-neuron-core IP macro** for licensing into MCU / SoC designs in standard 130 nm CMOS. Drivers: no on-chip learning / routing fabric (so not a Loihi competitor — building block, not full processor); standard-logic process is the unique angle vs Mythic / Syntiant / BrainChip Akida (all custom flows); slide-deck KPIs (pJ/spike, µm²/neuron, 100 % yield in 1T variant) read as an IP datasheet, not a chip spec.

Two strongest near-term applications: (1) edge AI co-processor for HDC / anomaly tasks (HDC headline applies directly); (2) physical RNG block (Bayesian RNG result applies directly). KWS is not demonstrated and should not be the lead pitch until the encoding question is solved.

## 6. Honest gaps — what we cannot do today

1. **Cell-wide DC < 1 dec at high gate overdrive.** ~5 dec plateau on V_G1 = 0.6 despite Vth + BJT-collector + Iii-path patches. Blocked on (a) deeper BSIM4 channel rewrite for high-overdrive, or (b) Sebas thick-ox card with properly extracted L = 0.13 µm IIMOD set (A.12 open).
2. **Transient validation (A.12).** Without V_d > 2 V multi-rate ramp data + wait-time experiment, z311 trap-reservoir predictions at 0.017 V/s and 1.7 V/s are unvalidated and snapback evidence stays slide-extracted (25–35 % per-sample uncertainty).
3. **KWS above chance.** DS-N1 failed honestly. Encoding-vs-physics question open; next steps = rank-coded inputs and non-spiking baseline at identical splits (4D-recheck).
4. **R-31 BBO terminated.** Final BBO on the post-Vth model gave eval 1 = 4.319 (worse than 4.21 baseline), eval 2 stalled, run killed. Fix-stacking has hit diminishing returns; further model work deferred pending A.12. Sub-1-dec cell-wide fit not expected without it.
5. **Snapback V_G2-slope sign inverted** (+0.611 vs −0.625 V/V). Likely root cause: missing distributed-body coupling and/or TAT contribution at V_d > 2 V.
6. **Code infrastructure debt.** `_residuals` does not consume `R_body distributed`, `avalanche M(V_bc)`, or `TAT block` cfg flags despite the BSIM cards shipping with calibrated TAT parameters. Multi-day implementation work; tracked separately.

## 7. NO-CHEAT discipline note (O64 critique, 2026-05-14)

A 3-way oracle critique (O64) reviewed the R-13 → R-31 arc and flagged several drifts. Documented and corrected:

- **Architecture "VINDICATED" overclaim (R-25):** pyport ≡ ngspice under identical OP shows pyport reproduces ngspice, not that the architecture is silicon-correct. v4.4 reports the status as "consistent with ngspice, channel evaluator 2–3 dec light at high overdrive".
- **Subset cherry-pick (z338 BBO):** the "floor ~3.43 dec" verdict was logged on a 9-bias subset with full-33 validation only added downstream (z339, which falsified the eta_sigmoid hypothesis at full 33). **v4.4 reports only full 33-curve evaluations.**
- **Post-hoc gates:** the model-fix-arc gate moved (< 0.95 → < 0.50 → sub-1.0 dec) across the night without pre-registration. Logged as process failure; R-30 onward pre-registers the gate before evaluating output.
- **"Honest FAIL glossed as expected":** R-21 / z337 "4.16 dec (worse, expected)" is reported here as a logged FAIL, not normalised regression.
- **z304 = 0.99 spurious claim:** rests on oracle consensus + BJT-fix-revert costing +1.8 dec. A targeted ablation (miswired BJT vs corrected BJT, identical params) would tighten the proof; on the post-v4.4 list.

Closed before v4.4: O44, O46, O49, O51, O54, O55. R-arc adds O63 (eta_sigmoid falsified) and O64 (overclaim + subset drift). All FAILs accepted on record.
